A Tribute to Nikola Tesla

Inventor, Visionary, and the Master of Electricity

A short summary on Nikola Tesla

  • Full Name: Nikola Tesla
  • Born: July 10, 1856, Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • Died: January 7, 1943, New York City, USA
  • Known for: AC electricity, Tesla Coil, wireless power, radio, and more.

Biography

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, engineer, and physicist. He is best known for his contributions to the development of alternating current (AC) electricity systems, which revolutionized how we power the world today. Born in 1856, Tesla was a visionary thinker who believed in free wireless power for all.
Tesla studied electrical engineering in Austria and later worked with Thomas Edison in the United States. However, their differing views on electricity led to the famous "War of Currents", where Tesla's AC system ultimately prevailed over Edison's DC system.
Tesla was granted over 300 patents during his lifetime. He pioneered technologies like wireless communication, X-rays, remote control, and even ideas related to radar and radio.

Timeline

  • 1856: Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire
  • 1884: Moved to the USA and started working with Thomas Edison
  • 1887: Developed AC induction motor and transformer
  • 1891: Invented the Tesla Coil
  • 1893: Demonstrated wireless electricity at the Chicago World's Fair
  • 1899: Built a wireless power lab in Colorado Springs
  • 1901-1906: Constructed Wardenclyffe Tower (failed wireless transmission project)
  • 1943: Died alone in New York City
